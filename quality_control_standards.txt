Document Title: Quality Control Standards for Production Lines
Document ID: QLT-001
Version: 1.0
Department: Quality Assurance

Purpose:
This document defines the inspection, acceptance, and process control requirements used to maintain product quality and compliance.

Scope:
Applies to incoming material checks, in-process inspection, and final product release.

Section 1: Incoming Material Inspection
Requirements:
1. Verify supplier label, batch number, and material code.
2. Check packaging integrity.
3. Inspect for visible damage, contamination, or moisture exposure.
4. Confirm quantity matches receiving records.
5. Record acceptance or rejection in the inspection log.

Reject incoming material if:
- Packaging is damaged
- Material identification is missing
- Certificate of conformance is absent when required
- Visible contamination is present

Section 2: In-Process Quality Checks
Operators and supervisors must verify:
- Correct machine settings are in use
- Product dimensions remain within tolerance
- Surface finish meets standard
- Labels and markings are correct
- No visible defects are present

Typical In-Process Defects:
- Scratches
- Incorrect dimensions
- Misalignment
- Surface contamination
- Incomplete assembly
- Wrong label

Section 3: Final Inspection Standard
Final inspection must confirm:
1. Product dimensions meet specification tolerance.
2. Appearance is free from critical defects.
3. Required labeling is present and legible.
4. Packaging is complete and correct.
5. Batch documentation is complete.

Defect Classification:
Critical Defect:
A defect that may create safety risk, regulatory noncompliance, or complete functional failure.
Action: Immediate rejection and supervisor escalation.

Major Defect:
A defect that affects performance, fit, or customer acceptance.
Action: Reject or hold for quality review.

Minor Defect:
A defect that does not affect primary function but does not fully meet cosmetic or workmanship standards.
Action: Record and evaluate against acceptance criteria.

Process Control Limits:
- If defect rate exceeds 2 percent in a production hour, notify quality and production supervision.
- If two consecutive samples fail dimensional inspection, stop the line and investigate.
- Any critical defect requires immediate containment of affected batch.

Supervisor Guidance:
When repeated defects are observed, isolate suspect material, inform quality assurance, and prevent further release until disposition is given.

Common Questions:
Q: What should happen if two back-to-back samples fail measurement checks?
A: The line should be stopped, the issue investigated, and quality assurance notified.

Q: Can product with a major defect be released with supervisor approval?
A: No. Product with a major defect must be rejected or formally reviewed by quality according to disposition rules.
