Document Title: General Safety Procedures for Plant Operations
Document ID: SAF-001
Version: 1.0
Department: Plant Safety

Purpose:
This document defines standard safety procedures for plant operators and floor supervisors to reduce risk of injury, equipment damage, and unsafe operating conditions.

Scope:
Applies to all personnel working in production, utility, maintenance support, and material handling areas.

General Safety Rules:
1. Wear required PPE at all times in designated production areas.
2. Never operate equipment unless trained and authorized.
3. Report unsafe conditions immediately to the floor supervisor.
4. Keep aisles, exits, and emergency equipment access points clear.
5. Do not bypass machine guards, interlocks, or emergency stops.
6. Follow lockout-tagout procedures before cleaning, inspection, or maintenance of equipment.
7. Use proper lifting techniques and approved lifting devices for heavy materials.
8. Immediately clean spills or isolate the area until it can be cleaned safely.
9. Do not enter confined spaces without permit approval and atmospheric testing.
10. Follow chemical handling instructions and review the Safety Data Sheet before use.

PPE Requirements:
- Safety helmet
- Safety glasses with side shields
- Steel-toe shoes
- Cut-resistant gloves when handling sharp parts or materials
- Hearing protection in marked high-noise areas
- Chemical-resistant gloves and goggles when handling chemicals

Emergency Response:
Fire:
- Activate the nearest alarm pull station
- Notify the supervisor
- Evacuate using the nearest safe exit
- Proceed to the assembly point
- Do not re-enter until clearance is given

Chemical Spill:
- Stop work immediately
- Isolate the area
- Inform the floor supervisor and EHS
- Use spill kits only if trained and if the spill is minor
- For major spills, evacuate and wait for the emergency response team

Machine Jam or Unsafe Condition:
- Stop the equipment using normal controls if safe
- Apply lockout-tagout before clearing the jam
- Never place hands near moving parts without verified zero-energy isolation

Supervisor Responsibilities:
- Confirm workers are using correct PPE
- Ensure pre-shift safety checks are completed
- Escalate serious hazards immediately
- Stop work when unsafe conditions exist
- Verify incident reporting is completed

Common Questions:
Q: Can an operator remove jammed material without lockout?
A: No. Any task requiring access to a danger zone must follow lockout-tagout.

Q: What should happen if a worker notices a chemical leak?
A: The worker should isolate the area, notify the supervisor, and follow the spill response process based on material risk.
