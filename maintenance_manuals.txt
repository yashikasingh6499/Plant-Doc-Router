Document Title: Maintenance Manual for Common Plant Equipment
Document ID: MNT-001
Version: 1.0
Department: Maintenance

Purpose:
This document provides standard troubleshooting and preventive maintenance guidance for commonly used plant equipment including pumps, conveyors, and sensors.

Scope:
Applies to maintenance technicians, reliability staff, and supervisors responsible for plant equipment upkeep.

Section 1: Pump Preventive Maintenance
Frequency:
- Visual inspection: daily
- Bearing temperature check: weekly
- Coupling inspection: monthly
- Seal inspection: monthly
- Full preventive maintenance review: quarterly

Pump Inspection Checklist:
1. Check for abnormal noise or vibration.
2. Inspect for seal leakage.
3. Verify suction and discharge pressure are within operating range.
4. Confirm motor current is within normal limits.
5. Check lubrication status of bearings if applicable.
6. Inspect coupling alignment.
7. Ensure mounting bolts are tight.

Common Pump Issues:
Issue: Low flow
Possible Causes:
- Blocked suction line
- Air ingress
- Worn impeller
- Low tank level

Issue: High vibration
Possible Causes:
- Misalignment
- Bearing wear
- Cavitation
- Loose base

Section 2: Conveyor Troubleshooting
Issue: Conveyor belt not starting
Checks:
1. Confirm power supply is available.
2. Verify emergency stop is reset.
3. Check overload relay status.
4. Inspect motor starter condition.
5. Confirm no jammed material is blocking motion.

Issue: Belt tracking to one side
Checks:
1. Inspect roller alignment.
2. Check belt tension.
3. Remove debris from rollers.
4. Verify frame is level.

Issue: Abnormal noise
Checks:
1. Inspect bearings for wear.
2. Check chain or drive tension.
3. Look for loose guards or panels.

Section 3: Sensor Calibration Guidance
Applies To:
- Temperature sensors
- Pressure transmitters
- Proximity sensors

Calibration Steps:
1. Isolate equipment if required.
2. Use calibrated reference instrument.
3. Record current reading.
4. Compare actual reading with reference value.
5. Adjust sensor offset if deviation exceeds tolerance.
6. Re-test after adjustment.
7. Record calibration results in the maintenance log.

Maintenance Rules:
- Only qualified personnel may open control panels.
- Lockout-tagout is required before invasive maintenance.
- Any repeated fault within 7 days must be escalated for root cause review.
- Replaced parts must match approved specifications.

Supervisor Guidance:
If the same conveyor trips multiple times in one shift, do not keep resetting without inspection. Escalate to maintenance for fault investigation.
